Historical Milestones in Women’s Football

  • 1921 - Ban on Women’s Football by The FA

    The Football Association in England bans women from playing on FA-affiliated pitches, a decision that hindered the growth of women’s football for 50 years.

  • 1996 - Women’s Football Debuts at the Olympics

    Women’s football was included in the Olympic Games for the first time, with the United States winning the gold medal in Atlanta, Georgia.

  • 2000 - Formation of the Women’s Professional Soccer League

    The Women’s Professional Soccer League (WPSL) was established, promoting the growth of women’s professional football in the United States.

  • 2015 - FIFA Women’s World Cup in Canada

    The FIFA Women’s World Cup was held in Canada, with the United States winning the championship and marking a significant moment in the tournament's history.

  • 2023 - Record Attendance at Women’s Football Match

    The UEFA Women's Champions League semifinal between Barcelona and Wolfsburg set a record for attendance at a women’s football match, with 91,648 fans at Camp Nou.
